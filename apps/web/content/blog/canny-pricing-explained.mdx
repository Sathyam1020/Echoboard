Canny's pricing page is straightforward at first glance. Free plan, Core ($19/mo), Pro ($79/mo), Business (custom). Click into the details and the picture changes — the headline number is just the entry price, and the actual cost depends on something most pricing pages bury: how many **tracked users** you have.

This post is a practical breakdown of what Canny actually costs, what a "tracked user" is, where the tier walls hit, and when you're better off looking at alternatives. Pricing data was verified on Canny's own pages on 2026-04-26.

<Callout variant="info" title="Why this matters">
Canny is a great product. The reason teams write blog posts like this one isn't

of getting surprised by a renewal email.

## What is a "tracked user"?

A tracked user in Canny is anyone who's interacted with your boards — voted, commented, or posted. The count is **rolling**: it includes everyone who's ever taken an action, not just monthly actives.

A few practical implications:

- **The number grows in only one direction.** A user who voted last year and never came back still counts.
- **Anonymous voters get tracked.** Even if Canny doesn't know who they are by name, the device gets counted as a tracked user.
- **You can purge inactive users**, but it's a manual operation buried in admin settings, and most teams don't do it.

For most SaaS teams, the tracked user count grows ~3x faster than monthly actives. That's the trap — the pricing tier you signed up at six months ago is no longer the tier you're actually on.

## The tier breakdown

### Free plan

- **Cost:** $0/mo
- **Tracked users:** 25
- **Boards:** 1
- **Includes:** Autopilot AI (deduplication), unlimited posts

The free plan is a true free tier in the sense that it's not time-limited, but the 25-tracked-user cap is small enough that any product with public engagement will hit it within weeks. It's best understood as a trial, not a long-term home.

### Core plan

- **Cost:** $19/mo (yearly) or $29/mo (monthly)
- **Tracked users:** 100 (additional packs available)
- **Boards:** 1
- **Includes:** Custom domain, public roadmap, integrations like Slack and Intercom

Core is where most SaaS teams start in earnest. The yearly price ($228/year) is reasonable. The catch is the 100-tracked-user cap — most teams blow through it within 60-90 days of going live. After that you're either buying overage packs or upgrading to Pro.

### Pro plan

- **Cost:** $79/mo (yearly) or $99/mo (monthly)
- **Tracked users:** 1,000 (additional packs available)
- **Boards:** Unlimited
- **Includes:** Jira, Linear, Asana, ClickUp, GitHub, Azure DevOps, Salesforce, HubSpot integrations + API + webhooks

Pro is the workhorse tier. $948/year. This is where Canny's product really shines — every meaningful integration unlocks here. It's also where most teams settle for the long haul, until tracked users push them past 1,000.

### Business plan

- **Cost:** Custom
- **Tracked users:** 5,000+
- **Boards:** Unlimited
- **Includes:** SSO/SAML, dedicated support, advanced segmentation

Business pricing is undisclosed publicly. Based on customer reports, it typically lands in the **$300-600/mo range** for teams in the 5,000-15,000 tracked-user band, with custom enterprise pricing above that. The jump from Pro to Business is the biggest pricing cliff in Canny's lineup.

## What it costs at every scale

Let's run the numbers on a realistic SaaS growth curve.

| Tracked users | Plan | Yearly cost |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 25 | Free | $0 |
| 100 | Core | $228 |
| 500 | Pro (under cap) | $948 |
| 1,000 | Pro (at cap) | $948 |
| 2,500 | Pro + overage | ~$1,400 |
| 5,000 | Business (entry) | ~$3,600 |
| 10,000 | Business | ~$5,000+ |

A SaaS that goes from launch to 5,000 tracked users in two years pays roughly **$5,000-7,000 to Canny across that period** — small relative to MRR if you're at $50k MRR, painful if you're at $5k MRR.

## Where the model breaks

Canny's pricing model is well-aligned for a specific type of product: B2B SaaS with **stable, paying customer counts** and **high MRR per user**. If you have 500 customers paying $200/mo each, paying Canny $948/year is a rounding error.

The model breaks for:

### Community-driven products

If most of your "tracked users" are not paying customers — they're free-tier users, prospects, or developers in your community — you're paying Canny for engagement that doesn't directly drive revenue. Common with devtools, OSS projects, and consumer apps.

### Pre-PMF startups

When you don't know if you'll have 50 or 5,000 customers next year, signing up for usage-based pricing is hard to budget for. The Core plan's 100-tracked-user cap is too tight; the Pro plan's $948/year is too expensive when you're not yet sure if the product will be alive in 18 months.

### Products with bursty traffic

Consumer apps that hit Product Hunt or a viral tweet can pick up thousands of new tracked users overnight. Most of them never come back, but they all count toward your tier forever.

## Mitigations within Canny

If you're committed to staying on Canny but want to control costs:

1. **Purge inactive tracked users quarterly.** It's tedious — there's no bulk "remove tracked users who haven't acted in 12 months" button — but it's free.
2. **Use private boards for internal feedback.** Internal users still count toward your tracked-user cap. Move internal feedback to Notion or a dedicated tool.
3. **Don't pre-identify everyone.** If your widget pre-identifies every signed-in user via JS, every signup becomes a tracked user. Pre-identify only when they take an action.
4. **Stay on Pro as long as possible.** The Pro-to-Business jump is the biggest pricing cliff. Buy overage packs to extend your time on Pro.

## When to look at alternatives

The decision tree is simple:

- **You're at fewer than 100 tracked users:** Stay on Canny Free or Core. The price-to-value is fine.
- **You're at 100-1,000 tracked users:** Canny Pro is reasonable if you need its integrations. If you don't, flat-rate alternatives are cheaper.
- **You're approaching 1,000-5,000:** Look at alternatives now. The Pro overage path or Business jump is where the math gets ugly.
- **You're past 5,000 and growing fast:** Almost any flat-rate alternative will save you money. The only reason to stay is integration depth or brand familiarity.

Canny's pricing is rational from Canny's perspective. They've optimized for B2B SaaS with stable user counts, where the per-tracked-user model captures fair value. If you're that customer, Canny is the safer, more mature pick.
