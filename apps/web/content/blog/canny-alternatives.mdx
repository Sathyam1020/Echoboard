Canny set the template for SaaS feedback management. Boards, voting, public roadmap, changelog — if you've used a SaaS product feedback tool in the last seven years, you've used Canny or a tool that copied its layout.

The trade-off is pricing. Canny charges per **tracked user** — the running total of every person who's interacted with your boards. The free plan caps at 25, Core ($19/mo) and Pro ($79/mo) start at 100, and Business is custom-priced for 5,000+. For a SaaS team that's already past their first 100 customers, Canny's price tag is in the same ballpark as a content marketing budget. For early-stage or community-driven products, that's hard to justify.

This piece walks through the 10 best Canny alternatives we found after researching every public competitor in the category. We've used each tool's own pricing pages as the source of truth and timestamped the data — pricing changes, so verify before committing.

## Why people look for Canny alternatives

The two reasons we hear most:

**Pricing scales the wrong way.** A SaaS with 1,000 monthly engaged users will outgrow Canny's Core plan and land on Pro at $79/mo (yearly billed). Add the Pro-only integrations (Jira, Linear, Slack), and you're at $948/year before SSO. Teams that don't have a stable user count find Canny's pricing especially painful — it's a rising cost as you succeed.

**Integrations and API are gated behind Pro.** If you want to sync feedback to Jira or post to Slack when a feature ships, you need Pro. The Core plan exists, but for many teams it doesn't have the integrations that make a feedback tool worth using day-to-day.

For teams that hit those walls, the alternatives split into four buckets: flat-rate hosted tools, all-in-one suites, open source self-hosted, and per-board tools.

## Flat-rate hosted alternatives

These tools charge a fixed monthly price regardless of how many users vote, comment, or submit feedback. Predictable budgets, no surprise bills.

### 1. EchoBoard

**Pricing:** Free forever / Pro $49/mo flat
**Best for:** SaaS teams of any size who want predictable pricing

EchoBoard is the tool we make. The free plan covers unlimited users, unlimited posts, public roadmap, changelog, and the embeddable widget — none of which Canny includes on its free tier. Pro at $49/mo flat adds multiple boards, custom domains, and removes EchoBoard branding.

The bet behind EchoBoard's pricing is simple: per-user pricing punishes growth. Your costs shouldn't go up just because more people care about your product. We get there by skipping a few enterprise features (SSO, Jira/Slack integrations, MRR-weighted voting) on day one and shipping them as we grow.

**Where it wins:** Free tier coverage, no-login feedback widget, flat pricing.
**Where it falls short:** Newer than the established players. Some integrations are still on the roadmap.

### 2. UserJot

**Pricing:** Free / Starter $29/mo / Professional $59/mo
**Best for:** Bootstrapped SaaS teams that want flat pricing with AI features

UserJot launched in late 2024 from solo founder Shayan Taslim (also creator of LogSnag). It has a generous free tier (unlimited users, 2 boards), flat-rate pricing, and built-in AI features for triage and summarization. Slack and Linear are first-class integrations.

The catch: the free plan doesn't include the widget or custom domains, and the Starter plan caps integrations at 1 — so teams that need both Slack and Linear have to upgrade to Professional.

**Where it wins:** AI features built in, native Linear integration on Starter.
**Where it falls short:** No anonymous posting (users need accounts), Jira not yet supported.

### 3. Upvoty

**Pricing:** Power $25/mo / Super $49/mo / Hyper $99/mo
**Best for:** Agencies and white-label SaaS that need deep custom CSS

Upvoty has been bootstrapped since 2018 and crossed $1M ARR within three years. It's the right pick if you want to **fully** rebrand the feedback experience — it ships custom HTML, custom CSS, and send-from-own-domain emails on every paid tier, which most other tools gate behind enterprise.

The downside: no permanent free plan (only a 14-day trial), and the lower tiers are limited to a single project.

**Where it wins:** White-labeling depth, custom SSO on every paid tier.
**Where it falls short:** No free plan, dated UI, no AI features.

## All-in-one suites

These tools bundle feedback with help center, surveys, or knowledge base — wider scope, higher complexity.

### 4. Featurebase

**Pricing:** Free / Growth $29/seat/mo / Professional $59/seat/mo / Enterprise $99/seat/mo (yearly)
**Best for:** Scaling SaaS teams that want feedback + help center + AI support in one suite

Featurebase is the most ambitious of the modern alternatives. Founded in 2020, it bundles feedback boards, public roadmaps, changelogs, surveys, a help center, and an AI-powered support inbox. It's also one of the only tools in the category that ships **MRR-weighted voting** out of the box via its Stripe and HubSpot integrations.

The trade-off is per-seat pricing. At Growth ($29/seat/mo), a 5-admin team pays $145/mo — manageable. At Professional ($59/seat/mo), the same team pays $295/mo. Add the $0.29-per-AI-resolution fees on top, and the bill compounds.

**Where it wins:** All-in-one breadth, MRR-weighted voting, mature AI agents.
**Where it falls short:** Per-seat pricing scales with team size, Slack integration listed as "coming soon."

### 5. ProductLift

**Pricing:** Starter from $19/mo / Pro from $49/mo / Business from $129/mo (yearly)
**Best for:** Mid-sized SaaS teams that want feedback + knowledge base + prioritization frameworks

ProductLift bundles feedback, roadmap, changelog, and a knowledge base. It supports 22 languages, ships RICE/ICE/MoSCoW prioritization frameworks natively, and includes monthly AI credits per plan (250 / 2,000 / 6,000). The flat-rate model with unlimited end-users is genuinely competitive.

The catch: no permanent free plan (14-day trial), and additional admins cost $15/mo each on top of the base subscription.

**Where it wins:** Built-in knowledge base, prioritization frameworks.
**Where it falls short:** No free plan, per-admin fees stack up.

### 6. FeatureOS (formerly Hellonext)

**Pricing:** Starter $50/mo / Growth $100/mo / Business $208/mo (yearly)
**Best for:** Mid-market SaaS that needs Salesforce integration

FeatureOS rebranded from Hellonext in 2023. It's an all-in-one suite (boards + roadmap + changelog + knowledge base + forms) with native Salesforce integration on the Business tier — useful if your sales team needs feature requests to flow into account records.

Pricing is flat per tier but with $15/mo fees per extra seat or board, which adds up.

**Where it wins:** Salesforce integration, AI features, mature product.
**Where it falls short:** No free plan, higher entry price than Sleekplan, recent rebrand still confuses users.

## Budget and indie alternatives

### 7. Sleekplan

**Pricing:** Indie free / Starter $13/mo / Business $38/mo (yearly)
**Best for:** Early-stage SaaS that wants NPS/CSAT surveys alongside feedback

Sleekplan is the cheapest paid tier in the category at $13/mo. It bundles feedback boards with NPS and CSAT surveys — useful if you want one tool for both. The Indie free plan is permanent and includes unlimited end-users, though the public roadmap kanban requires Starter or higher.

**Where it wins:** Lowest paid tier, surveys included, permanent free plan.
**Where it falls short:** Custom domains gated to Business, no MRR-weighted voting.

### 8. Nolt

**Pricing:** Essential $29/mo / Pro $69/mo / Enterprise custom
**Best for:** Indie SaaS, gaming studios, and Discord-driven communities

Nolt has been a category staple since 2018. It's deliberately narrow — just feedback boards and a roadmap, no changelog, no AI. The clean Trello-style UI is a strong fit for community-driven products where you want users to grasp the interface immediately.

The catch: no permanent free plan (10-day trial), and the Essential tier is single-board.

**Where it wins:** Mature Jira/Slack/Linear integrations, anonymous voting toggle, rock-stable bootstrapped company.
**Where it falls short:** No free plan, no native changelog, no AI features.

### 9. Feature Upvote

**Pricing:** Indie $49/board/mo / Standard $99/board/mo / Enterprise custom
**Best for:** Game studios and B2B SaaS that need a single board with SAML SSO

Feature Upvote has been bootstrapped since 2016 by Steve McLeod, who's been public about preferring a calm, profitable business over aggressive growth. Translation: stable, low-churn, slow feature velocity. It's also one of the only tools in the category that includes SAML SSO on every paid tier.

The cost model is per-board, which means multiple products linearly multiply the bill.

**Where it wins:** SAML SSO included on all paid tiers, very stable bootstrapped company.
**Where it falls short:** Per-board pricing for multi-product teams, no changelog or AI features.

## Open source

### 10. Fider

**Pricing:** Free (self-hosted) / $49/mo (Pro cloud)
**Best for:** Privacy-conscious teams who want to fully own their feedback data

Fider is the open-source feedback platform. It's been actively maintained since 2016, ships as a Go application with Postgres, and is GDPR-friendly thanks to self-hosting. The cloud-hosted Pro tier at $49/mo is fair-priced; the self-hosted version is unconditionally free.

The trade-off: no roadmap, no changelog, no widget, no AI features. Fider does the core feedback flow (boards, voting, comments, statuses) and stops there. Most teams using Fider pair it with a separate changelog tool.

**Where it wins:** Open source, self-hostable, full data ownership.
**Where it falls short:** Smaller feature surface, no first-party integrations, dated UI.

## How to pick

Match the tool to your constraints:

- **You want predictable pricing as you scale:** EchoBoard, UserJot, Upvoty, Nolt
- **You want everything in one tool (feedback + help center + AI):** Featurebase, FeatureOS, ProductLift
- **You want the cheapest paid tier:** Sleekplan ($13/mo)
- **You need self-hosting for compliance:** Fider
- **You want bootstrapped stability over feature velocity:** Feature Upvote, Nolt
- **You're an agency reselling feedback boards:** Upvoty
- **You need Salesforce integration:** Featurebase or FeatureOS

The market for Canny alternatives is bigger than it looks — there are credible options at every price point and feature depth. The wrong move is sticking with a tool whose pricing model doesn't match how your business grows.
